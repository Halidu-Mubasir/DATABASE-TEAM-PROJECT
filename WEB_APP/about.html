CASE STUDY: NORTHERN ELECTRICITY DISTRIBUTION COMPANY (NEDCo)

Northern Electricity Distribution Company (NEDCo) was established to distribute electricity in the Brong-Ahafo, Northern,
Upper East and Upper West Regions of Ghana as part of VRA’s 161kV transmission grid extension to the northern parts of Ghana.
NEDCo's current operations extend to the northern parts of Oti, Ashanti and Western North regions. Although NEDCo’s operations
cover about 64% of the geographical area of Ghana, the customer density of the operating area is low with access to electricity
in the NEDCo operational area put at 65% as at December 2017 as against about 84% for the whole country. The Ghana Government,
in line with its vision of making electricity available to at least, 90% of the population by 2020, has undertaken power extension
over the years to new towns and communities that were hitherto not served by NEDCo. The customer population has, therefore, grown at
an average rate of about 13% plus per annum from less than 20,000 in 1987 to 888,023 by March 2019. Residential customers
constitute 81.39% of the total customers; Non-Residential customers constitute 18.60% while the Special Load Tariff (SLT)
customers constitute only 0.01%.


OPERATIONS

NEDCo's operations in the regions are managed by five branch offices designated as Area Offices in the following cities:
Sunyani, Techiman, Tamale, Wa and Bolgatanga. The head office of NEDCo is strategically located in Tamale, the Capital of
the Northern Region. The Head Office administration is now headed by a Managing Director, supported by five functional Managers namely
Technical, Human Resource, Finance, Commercial and Real Estate Managers. There is also an Audit Head who reports administratively to
the Managing Director. Each Area is headed by a manager supported by an Engineer, Finance Officer, Commercial Officer, IT Officer and
a Human Resource Officer.



REASON FOR THIS PROJECT

The Northern Electricity Department (NED) of the Volta River Authority (VRA) distributes electricity
in the Brong-Ahafo, Northern, Upper East, Upper West, Savannah, Bono, Bon East, Ashanti, Oti and Western regions.
The workforce aspires to be a self-sustaining electric power distribution company and a safe and reliable
supplier of electricity to homes and businesses in a commercially viable manner. This cannot be achieved when there are inefficiencies.
NEDCO finds itself in a situation of paying tariffs. Some Customers are not paying the entirety of the electricity consumed for example
through mistakes in the bills, failure to pay or illegal power tapping. Management would like to have information about accurate bills in the various regions.
Managers would also like to keep records of customers who are indebted across the various branches as well as the transactions that happen
between them and their suppliers across the countries of their scope.
